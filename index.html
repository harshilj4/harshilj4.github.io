ABOUT THE BUILDER

CURRENT VECTOR // HARDWARE · SENSORS · FLIGHT

I’m a mechanical engineering student who learns fastest when the idea has to survive contact with real hardware.

My strongest interests sit where mechanical design, electronics, and testing overlap: building prototypes, collecting useful data, and improving the system until it behaves the way it should. I’m especially drawn to aerospace, automotive systems, manufacturing, and instrumentation because they reward precision, iteration, and proof over theory alone.

Right now, my research direction is focused on laser-induced graphene sensors for strain, temperature, and humidity. That means thinking about how material preparation, packaging, and measurement setup all work together to turn physical changes into signals you can trust.

Engineering style: start with first principles, build the rough version, test honestly, fix what failed, document the change, then repeat until the system earns confidence.
